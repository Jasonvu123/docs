---
title: "Watchlist"
description: "Save and monitor your favorite markets."
---

The watchlist lets you bookmark markets you want to follow. Watchlisted markets appear as a horizontal row of cards on your dashboard so you can check their current probability and price change at a glance without navigating to each market individually.

Your watchlist is saved to your account and persists across sessions and devices.

## Adding a market to your watchlist

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find a market">
    Browse the market feed, use the search bar, or look through the Trending, Biggest Movers, or Ending Soon sections on your dashboard.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click the star icon">
    On any market card, click the star icon in the corner. The star turns yellow to confirm the market has been added.
  </Step>
  <Step title="View on your dashboard">
    Return to your dashboard to see the market appear in the **Watchlist** row at the top of the page.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Viewing your watchlist

Your watchlisted markets appear as a row of cards directly on the dashboard, above the Trending and Biggest Movers sections. Each card shows:

- The market title and image
- The current YES probability as a percentage
- The 24-hour price change (green for up, red for down)
- A filled yellow star confirming it's saved

Click any card to go directly to that market's trade page.

## Removing a market from your watchlist

Click the yellow star icon on any watchlisted market card — on the dashboard or in the main market feed — to remove it from your watchlist. The star returns to its unselected state and the card fades out of the watchlist row.

## What the watchlist shows

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Title** | The market question |
| **YES probability** | Current implied probability, updated every 60 seconds |
| **24h change** | Price movement over the last 24 hours |

<Tip>
Use the watchlist to track a small set of high-conviction markets without losing them in the full feed. The dashboard watchlist row is always visible at a glance when you first log in.
</Tip>
