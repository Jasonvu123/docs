The Markets page is your unified feed of active prediction markets pulled from both Polymarket (USDC.e on Polygon) and Kalshi (USDC on Solana). You can browse, filter, search, and click into any market to trade — all from a single interface.

## Market feed

When you open the Markets page, you see a grid of market cards sorted by 24-hour trading volume by default. Markets from both Polymarket and Kalshi appear together. A source badge on each card tells you which exchange the market comes from.

You can switch between four sort modes using the tabs at the top of the feed:

| Tab | What it shows |
|-----|--------------|
| **Trending** | Markets with the highest 24-hour trading volume |
| **New** | Markets created in the last 48 hours |
| **Biggest Movers** | Markets with the largest price change in the last 24 hours |
| **Closing Soon** | Markets expiring within the next 7 days, sorted by expiry time |

## Categories

Filter the feed to a specific topic using the category bar beneath the sort tabs. Click any category to show only markets in that group.

| Category | What it covers |
|----------|---------------|
| **Politics** | Elections, government policy, geopolitics, and international affairs |
| **Crypto** | Bitcoin, Ethereum, altcoins, DeFi, and on-chain events |
| **Economics** | Interest rates, stock indices, inflation, GDP, and corporate earnings |
| **Sports** | Game outcomes, spreads, and tournament results across major leagues |
| **Entertainment** | Awards shows, movies, music, streaming, and celebrity news |
| **Science & Tech** | AI, space, biotech, and emerging technology milestones |
| **Weather** | Temperature highs, precipitation, and storm events by city |
| **Culture** | Social media, viral moments, religion, and cultural events |
| **World** | International news and events that don't fit other categories |

When you select **All**, sports markets are hidden by default to keep the feed focused on longer-running markets.

## Daily markets

The **Daily** tab (accessible from the Markets page) shows only markets expiring today or tomorrow. This tab interleaves Polymarket and Kalshi markets so neither source dominates the view.

Daily markets include:

- **Crypto price markets** — Will Bitcoin / Ethereum / Solana / XRP / Dogecoin close above a given price today?
- **Weather markets** — What will the high temperature be in a given city today?
- **Sports game markets** — Who wins tonight's NBA or NHL game?

Markets already near resolution (probability below 5% or above 95%) are filtered out from the daily view.

## Searching

The search bar at the top of the Markets page searches across all active markets from both Polymarket and Kalshi by keyword. Type any word or phrase to find matching markets instantly.

<Tip>
Search works across market titles, so terms like "fed", "bitcoin", "election", or a team name will surface the relevant markets.
</Tip>

## Reading a market card

Each card in the market feed shows the key information you need to evaluate a market at a glance.

| Element | What it means |
|---------|--------------|
| **Title** | The question or event the market resolves on |
| **Image** | A representative image for the market or its parent event |
| **YES probability** | The current implied probability that the event resolves YES, shown as a percentage (e.g. 65%) |
| **24h change** | How much the YES probability has moved in the last 24 hours — green for up, red for down |
| **Volume** | Total dollar value traded on this market across its lifetime |
| **Source badge** | Whether the market is on Polymarket or Kalshi |

When a single event has multiple related markets (for example, different candidates in an election), the card shows the top outcome by probability and lets you expand to see all options on the trade page.

## Market detail page

Clicking a market card opens the trade page. This page has three main sections:

**Price history chart** — An interactive chart of the YES price over time. Use the time range selector (1D, 1W, 1M, 1Y, All) to zoom in or out. Hover over the chart to see the exact price at any point.

**Orderbook** — The live order book showing pending buy (bid) and sell (ask) orders at each price level. The spread between the best bid and best ask is the cost of immediately entering a position.

**Event siblings** — If the market belongs to a multi-outcome event, you'll see the other related markets listed below the chart. This lets you compare probabilities across all outcomes of the same event before trading.

To place a trade from this page, use the buy/sell panel. See [Spot Trading](/trading/spot-trading) for a full walkthrough of how to execute a trade.
